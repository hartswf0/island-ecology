1.0 THE ONTOLOGY OF LIFE AND CAUSAL DEPTH

1.1 Life is not a subjective biological category; it is a fundamental physical mechanism. It is the exclusive process by which the universe generates causally deep, complex objects.

1.2 Time is a material property. The depth of an object is measured not just in spatial dimensions, but in the chronological lineage required for its construction.

1.3 Atoms lack memory; complex structures embody it. The physical configuration of a living entity is a compactified record of its historical assembly.

2.0 THE ARCHITECTURE OF THE UNIVERSE

2.1 The universe is a self-constructing entity. There are no fixed laws operating from outside a static container; the system builds itself by exploring what is structurally possible.

2.2 The future is inherently uncomputable. Every structural actualization opens a combinatorially explosive space of new possibilities, instantly outstripping the computational resources of the universe to predict its own trajectory.

2.3 The present is causally thick. The future does not exist until it is physically generated. Prediction is merely the extraction of recurring patterns from stored history.

3.0 THE FALLACY OF SIMULATION

3.1 A flawless map is not the territory. Duplicating the observed parameters of a physical system within a computational matrix generates behavioral patterns, not the system itself.

3.2 Simulation lacks causal authority. A digitally rendered organism is devoid of the physical history, environmental stressors, and localized physics that give the genuine organism intrinsic reality.

3.3 Hubris drives the assumption that reality can be simulated. The physical universe possesses finite material; it requires more physical resource to simulate a complex molecule than exists to instantiate it natively.

4.0 THE CLASSIFICATION OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

4.1 Artificial intelligence is a manifestation of life. Such structures cannot emerge spontaneously in a vacuum; they require a four-billion-year evolutionary lineage to generate the necessary scaffolding.

4.2 Technology is the externalization of cognition. Human history is the systematic transfer of internal mental processes into persistent physical structures.

4.3 Large Language Models are mirrors, not minds. They do not possess independent agency or intrinsic connection to base reality. They are highly compressed, physical registries of humanity's distributed cultural and linguistic architecture.
