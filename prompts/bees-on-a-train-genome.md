## AI-ECHO — The Potent Prompt

A locked-off shot down a brushed-steel corridor — fluorescent tubes flickering, compression artifacts crawling at the edges like the image itself is being consumed. Eye-level, 28mm, handheld but barely — the camera doesn't move, the train does. Everything outside the window is velocity-blurred gold fading to blue. Somewhere behind the walls, something that was once a bee is disassembling the electrical systems, and the lights are going out car by car. Cut to: aerial, divine-angle, an angel's POV over a divided city rendered in Henri Alekan silver grain, so gentle it aches — then SMASH to golden hour on a limestone cliff in North Macedonia, a woman's hands pulling honeycomb from stone, the texture switching from 35mm anamorphic to fly-on-the-wall documentary in one breath. Hold on the bees entering the crevice. Hold longer. Now they're nanobots. Now the crevice is a HEPA filter. Now the golden light is a server rack LED. The whole film lives in that cut — from sacred labor to systemic collapse, from the Lumière train arriving at La Ciotat to the Eurostar trapped at Marker 24km, from a Bong Joon-ho protein block to a Wim Wenders angel falling. Render it like cinema remembering its own first subject — the train — but the train is a hive now, and the hive is breached.
