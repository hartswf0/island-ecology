  The Achilles RLM Engines operate strictly as a closed Language Game.

The FieldKernel serves as the inherited background, the non-negotiable bedrock of physics that stands fast. Its coordinate graphs and scalars are not hypotheses to be tested by the nodes—they are the rules that make the nodes' speech possible.

  Inside the NodeLens, there is no infinite regress of "why does this node feel this way?" We hit bedrock instantly. The node receives a scalar profile (`bloom`, `fracture`, `outage`) and translates it into an unassailable declaration: "Cold light opens in me." A mistake here is logically inconceivable because the system does not ask the node what is true; it asks the node to execute a tone of voice.

  Similarly, the EcoLens functions without attempting to justify the ecological reality of the board. It does not waste computational energy attempting to prove the field's imbalance. It accepts the `resonanceScore` as a hinge proposition, turning entirely toward the active variable: the deployment of `counterpoint`, `harmonize`, or `stabilize`. The analysis is entirely operational.

  By eradicating the philosophical attempt to make the simulation "aware" of itself, both the engine and the cheap variant achieve high-speed transmission. The architecture relies purely on the practical, ungrounded ways of acting that define the Worldtext. The simulation exists because the game is played, not because the game can be proven.
